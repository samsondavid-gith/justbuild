Pitch deck prompts

Four prompts for your final office hours session. The deck you build today is the deck you pitch on Demo Day.

How to use this page. Keep this tab open during the office hours call. When the facilitator says to use a prompt, scroll to it, click Copy, then paste into the right tool in another tab.
Step 1 · 15 min Brainstorm

Pick your venture name

Brainstorm 5 options using three approaches. Read aloud. Pick the one that makes you smile.

Spend 15 minutes brainstorming a name for YOUR specific venture. Not the generic idea name (Tiffin Choice, Rent a Tree etc). A name only you would pick. Three ways to find a name: 1. The PERSON name approach: Your name + what you do. 'Padma's Tiffin', 'Karthik Curates', 'Anjali Bakes'. 2. The PLACE name approach: Where it lives. 'BalconyBuds', 'Indiranagar Tiffin', 'Bagru Block'. 3. The FEELING name approach: What it gives the customer. 'Fresh Friday', 'Slow Plants', 'Steady Mornings'. Write 5 options. Read them aloud. Pick the one that makes you smile. That is your venture name.
Step 2 · 20 min Canva

Design your logo

Canva template, drop in your venture name, choose a colour, download as PNG with transparent background.

Open Canva. Free account. 1. Search 'logo' in the Canva templates. 2. Filter by SIMPLE / MINIMAL designs. Avoid templates with too many elements. 3. Replace the placeholder name with YOUR venture name from Step 1. 4. Pick a colour that matches your venture's feeling. Warm orange for plants. Mustard yellow for food. Deep green for farms. Pink for events. 5. Download as PNG with transparent background. Save it. You will use it in the deck now and on your website later this week.

Step 3 · 15 min Google Doc

Answer six questions

2 lines each in a Google Doc. These answers go straight into the deck prompt next.

Answer these six questions in 2 lines each. Write them in a Google Doc. These answers go straight into your deck prompt. 1. WHO ARE YOU? Your name, your college, your year. 2. WHAT IS YOUR VENTURE CALLED? The name from Step 1. 3. WHAT IS YOUR ONE-LINE TAGLINE? What your venture does in 7 words or less. Example: 'Rent a tree. Eat the fruit for a season.' 4. WHO IS YOUR CUSTOMER? The real person who paid (or said they would pay). Name them. Where they live. What they do. 5. WHAT PROBLEM DID YOU SOLVE FOR THEM? In one sentence. 6. WHAT IS ONE REAL VALIDATION INSIGHT? What is one thing a real user said or did that proved the venture works? Quote them if you can.
Step 4 · 40 min Gamma

Build the deck in Gamma

Paste this with your six answers from Step 3. Six-slide deck in one shot. Upload your logo to slide 1.

Build me a 6-slide pitch deck for my college micro-venture. I am a final-year engineering student. [ANSWER 1: WHO ARE YOU] MY VENTURE IS CALLED: [ANSWER 2] TAGLINE: [ANSWER 3] MY CUSTOMER: [ANSWER 4] PROBLEM I SOLVED: [ANSWER 5] REAL VALIDATION INSIGHT: [ANSWER 6] My app is live at: [YOUR APP URL] My website is live at: [YOUR WEBSITE URL] Make the deck warm, simple, and visual. Not corporate. Six slides: 1. TITLE: My name, my venture name, my tagline. Reserve space for me to upload my logo. 2. THE PROBLEM: Who has it, the real customer from above, one specific example. 3. THE INSIGHT: What real user behaviour or quote proved the venture works. 4. WHAT I BUILT: Show that I have an app, a website, a chatbot, and an agent. Add URLs. 5. HOW IT WORKS: Three simple steps the customer takes. 6. WHAT COULD HAPPEN NEXT: If 20 paying customers used this, what would change? Use warm colours. Big visuals. Short text. The kind of deck a founder would present at Demo Day to early customers, friends, and faculty, not to a VC.
