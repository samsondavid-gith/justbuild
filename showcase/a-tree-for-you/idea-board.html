A Tree for You

a venture pressure test, not a pep talk
Honest
not a cheerleader
The person

Meera Iyer, 35, product manager in Bengaluru, HSR Layout. Grew up in Ratnagiri district where her family had Alphonso trees. Summers meant eating mangoes off their own tree. Now in a flat with a six-year-old.

The moment it bites

Every April she orders an Alphonso box online for about ₹2,200. Half arrive bruised or taste flat and clearly force-ripened. She feels cheated, and a little sad that her daughter thinks mangoes come from a Zepto box.

What she does today

Buys a premium Alphonso box online each season, quality hit-or-miss, or waits for relatives to send a crate from the Konkan.

What I will build

A web app where Meera adopts one real Alphonso tree on a named Konkan farm for the season, sees photo updates, and gets that tree's naturally ripened fruit when it is ready.

1Is the problem real?

The pain is documented. Farm-direct sellers exist precisely because market mangoes are often picked unripe and force-ripened with chemicals, arriving hard, uniformly yellow and with no aroma.

Trust is broken too. A Ratnagiri farm warns that most Alphonso sold under the Ratnagiri name is not the real thing.

The nostalgia is real and it sells. Sellers lean on the Konkan flavour families tie to childhood summers, and on festival and corporate gifting.

Honest flag

This is a seasonal, premium, emotional want, not a daily pain. Meera is fine without it. That makes it a softer, weaker problem than food or rent. Respect that.

3 questions for Meera
About last time, not "would you use an app"
  1. Show me your last two mango or premium-fruit orders. What did you pay, and what actually arrived?
  2. Last summer, did you reorder from the same seller or switch? Why?
  3. When relatives send fruit from back home, how does that feel compared to the box you bought online?

3Can a student build it in six weeks?
Smallest version

One page. A handful of real trees on ONE partner farm, each with a photo, the variety and the farmer's name. Meera picks one, pays a deposit, and gets a simple updates page.

Build for real

Tree list from a simple data file: id, photo, variety, price. An adoption form: name, phone, city, tree. A confirmation screen. Orders into a sheet or your inbox.

Fake or do by hand

Payment: a UPI deposit by hand, no gateway. Farming: partner with ONE real farm that already grows the trees. Updates: photos you post manually from WhatsApp. Delivery: local pickup or same-city courier.

Cut for now

No pan-India shipping, no second farm, no multiple varieties, no live tracking, no login, no app store. One farm, one season.

Reality check

The app is easy. The venture is two hard things: locking a real farm before the season, and the timing. Mango season is a fixed window. If your six weeks miss it, you cannot deliver. Pick a crop and season you can fulfil now.

4The money on one customer
What she pays

An adoption sells for about ₹2,000 to ₹3,500 a tree, with Karpe charging ₹2,000 a year. A premium Alphonso box is ₹1,800 to ₹2,900 a dozen.

What it costs me
  • Farmer's share for the tree's care and harvest
  • Breathable packing boxes
  • Same-city courier: ₹100 to 300
  • Pan-India express or air: ₹500 to 1,500, plus spoilage
The math

Ship pan-India and air freight plus spoilage can eat a ₹2,500 tree alive, and one bruised box means a refund. Stay same-city or pickup and costs drop to ₹100 to 300, so ₹2,500 can clear a real margin after the farmer's share.

Hard truth

The unit only works LOCAL. One customer near one farm. Perishable fruit across India is a logistics business, not a student pilot.

5Why now?

Free AI tools let you build the app and the updates page in days, and UPI lets you collect deposits with no gateway.

Farm-direct, traceable, naturally ripened fruit is a proven and growing want. A funded startup already validates the model.

It is strictly seasonal. You must build and sell inside the mango window, not whenever you feel ready.

Honest flag

That funded competitor is also the warning. Why now for YOU is weak unless you go hyperlocal and sell THIS season. The opportunity is old. The only fresh angle is one farm, one city, right now.

6The riskiest assumption
The belief that kills it

That strangers will pre-pay ₹2,000 plus, months ahead, for one specific tree's fruit, from an unknown student, sight unseen. And that you can get ripe fruit to them intact at a price that still leaves margin.

Test it this week

Lock ONE real farm willing to let you sell its trees this season. Then pre-sell 5 adoptions, or 5 boxes from one named tree, to real people who pay a ₹500 deposit now.

Win condition

A farm partner says yes, and 5 strangers pay a ₹500 deposit for fruit that arrives months later. If they will not pre-pay, the trust is not there, and an app will not create it.

The verdict
1

Validated but crowded. A funded startup, Rent A Tree, already does exactly this across three states. That is the warning as much as the proof: do not try to be a pan-India shipper, you will lose on logistics.

2

Build it if you change one thing: go radically hyperlocal. One real farm, one variety, one city, same-city pickup or delivery, sold this season. The app is easy; the farm partnership and the season window are the real venture.

3

If you cannot lock a farm and collect 5 deposits this week, swap to a sharper version: pre-order a box from one named tree on one named farm, for a single city, with a photo and the farmer's name. It proves the story sells, with none of the pan-India shipping risk.

Better to hear it now than on Demo Day.
