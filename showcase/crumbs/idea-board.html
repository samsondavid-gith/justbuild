The person

Sneha, 29, works in Indiranagar, Bengaluru. Her mum's birthday is this Saturday. She wants a fresh, personal cake, not the same design everyone else gets.

The moment it bites

It is Thursday night. She is scrolling Bakingo and two Instagram home bakers. The chain cakes look identical. The home baker has no menu, no price, and has not replied to her DM. Saturday is stressing her out.

What she does today

Orders a same-day cake from Bakingo or FlowerAura for ₹500 to ₹900, or gambles on an Instagram home baker over DM.

What I will build

An order pad app for one neighbourhood bakery: see today's bakes and cake options, pick a size and date, add a message, choose pickup or delivery, and get a clear price and confirmation.

1Is the problem real?

The pain is real and named. The big online bakeries themselves joke that people are still ordering just vanilla. Generic is the default.

Demand is huge and growing. India has about 60 million birthdays a year, and the cake market is growing about 13 percent, led by customized cakes.

Eggless is underserved. Roughly half of Indian cake buyers want eggless, and the chains treat it as an afterthought.

Honest flag

Convenience is mostly solved. Bakingo delivers same-day from ₹379. Crumbs is not faster or cheaper. It must win on fresh, personal and reliable, or it loses.

3Can a student build it in six weeks?
Smallest version

One page. Today's bakes and a few cake options, each with a photo, size and price. Sneha picks one, sets a date and message, and gets a confirmation.

Build for real

A menu from a simple data file. An order form: item, size, date, message, pickup or delivery, name and phone. Orders into a sheet or your inbox.

Fake or do by hand

Payment: cash or UPI on pickup, no gateway. Baking: you, or one real partner bakery. Delivery: pickup or a nearby drop you do yourself. Menu updated by hand each morning.

Cut for now

No live tracking, no accounts, no app store, no city-wide delivery, no payment gateway. One bakery, one neighbourhood.

Reality check

The app is easy. The venture is the baking: can you bake fresh, on time, every day, and nail a custom cake for a fixed date? That is a kitchen and reliability problem, not a code problem.

4The money on one customer
What she pays

A celebration cake is about ₹500 to ₹1,200. Daily bakes are ₹30 to ₹120. Bakingo starts at ₹379.

What one cake costs me
  • Ingredients for a 1kg cake: about ₹250
  • Box and board: about ₹40
  • Your time: real, the hidden cost
  • Nearby delivery: ₹0 to 60, or free pickup
The math

A ₹900 cake can keep ₹500 plus after materials, and pickup keeps delivery near zero. Daily bread is thin margin but brings the same people back weekly. Cakes are the profit, bread is the habit.

Hard truth

The cake margin is real only if you can bake reliably from a small kitchen. One missed Saturday cake and you lose the customer and the word of mouth.

5Why now?

Free AI tools build the order pad and menu in days, and UPI collects payment with no gateway.

Quick-commerce trained people to order cakes online. Now they want fresh and personal too, not just fast.

Customized and eggless cakes are the fastest-growing, highest-margin slice of a growing market.

Honest flag

The market is old and crowded with funded chains. Why now for YOU is being the local, reliable, made-to-order bakery the chains cannot be, not out-marketing them.

6The riskiest assumption
The belief that kills it

That you can reliably bake fresh daily bakes AND deliver a perfect custom cake on the exact date, every time, from a small kitchen, while keeping the order pad full.

Test it this week

Take 5 real cake orders for this week from neighbours, at a real price, with real dates. Bake and deliver all 5 on time.

Win condition

5 cakes delivered on time, and at least 3 people reorder or send a friend. If you cannot reliably deliver 5, the venture is a kitchen problem to fix before any app.

The verdict
1

Real demand, crowded shelf. Birthdays and cakes are a big, growing market, but Bakingo, FlowerAura and the chains own convenience. You cannot win on faster or cheaper.

2

Build it, and win on the one thing they cannot copy: a real neighbourhood bakery that is as personal as an Instagram baker and as reliable as a chain. Fresh daily, made to order, a clear order pad, pickup or nearby delivery.

3

The app is the easy 20 percent. Prove the hard 80 percent first: bake and deliver 5 real cakes on time this week. If the kitchen and reliability hold, the order pad turns it into a business.

Better to hear it now than on Demo Day.
