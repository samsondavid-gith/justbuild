How it works

Good food should be simple to get.

No long forms, no fixed thali you did not ask for. You choose, we cook fresh, it reaches you hot. Here is the whole thing.

01

You pick your meals

Open this week's menu and choose what you feel like, veg or non veg. A fresh list goes up every day, so you are never stuck eating the same thing. Pick for tomorrow, or for the whole week.

02

We cook it that morning

Your food is made the same day, in small batches, the way it is cooked at home. Nothing sits in a fridge for three days. Nothing is reheated from a tray cooked last week.

03

It reaches you hot

Packed warm in a clean steel dabba and delivered to your desk or doorstep inside the lunch window. You open the lid and it still feels like it just came off the stove.

04

Skip the days you do not need

Going home for the weekend, or eating out with friends? Skip that day in a tap. You only pay for the dabbas you actually take, and you can pause the whole thing whenever life gets busy.

Good to know

Questions, answered.

Can I really skip days?+
Yes, any day you like. Skip before the morning cut off and you are not charged for that dabba. Travelling for a week? Pause the whole plan and start again when you are back.
Is the food veg or non veg?+
Both. Every day's menu has veg and non veg meals, clearly marked, and you choose meal by meal. A pure veg week and a mixed week are both completely fine.
How fresh is it, really?+
Cooked the same morning it reaches you, in small batches. We do not cook for the week and reheat. If it would not be good enough for our own family's lunch, it does not go in the dabba.
What if I do not like a meal?+
Tell us. We would rather fix it than lose you. Genuine issues with a dabba are made right, no long arguments.
Is there a long contract?+
No lock in at all. Pause or stop whenever you want. We would rather earn your next dabba than trap you in a year of them.
