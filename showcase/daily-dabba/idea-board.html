The person

Ravi Kulkarni, 23. First IT job in Bellandur. Moved from Belagavi to Bengaluru four months ago. Lives in a PG in HSR Layout. No kitchen, no time, never learned to cook.

The moment it bites

8:40 pm, back in the PG, drained. Lunch was already outside food. He opens Swiggy, scrolls for ten minutes, it all looks oily or costs ₹250 plus. He just wants simple ghar ka khana.

What he does today

Re-orders the same three or four Swiggy places, or eats bland PG mess food he is sick of. Maggi on the bad nights.

What I will build

A simple web app where Ravi picks home-style meals for the week from a small daily menu, cooked fresh that morning and dropped to his PG, with the freedom to skip any day.

1Is the problem real?

The pain is named out loud. A Bengaluru tiffin roundup opens by asking if you are tired of the Swiggy and Zomato scroll every single day.

Demand is steady, not a fad. Justdial lists 5,800 plus tiffin services in Bengaluru, and Sulekha logged 90 plus enquiries in two months.

Even Swiggy sees it. It launched EatRight because people increasingly want healthier food than the usual delivery plate.

3Can a student build it in six weeks?
Smallest version

One page. This week's menu, one veg and one non veg per day. Ravi ticks the days he wants, sees a total, and submits. That is the whole product.

Build for real

Menu from a simple data file you edit weekly. An order form: name, PG, phone, days, veg or non veg. Orders drop into a Google Sheet or your inbox. A clear confirmation screen.

Fake or do by hand

Payment: cash or UPI on delivery, no gateway. Cooking: one real home cook, or your mum, for the pilot. Delivery: you on a scooter. Updates: a WhatsApp "out for delivery".

Cut for now

No login, no ratings, no second kitchen, no second area, no auto-renew, no app store. One lane, one cook, one weekly cycle.

Reality check

The app is easily a six-week build. The real work is the kitchen plus delivery for even 5 to 10 real people. Do not hide in the code.

4The money on one customer
What he pays

Market is ₹80 to ₹150 a meal. Monthly plans run ₹2,500 to ₹3,200, about ₹85 to ₹110 a meal. Pitch around ₹120 for a fresh home-style dinner.

What one meal costs me
  • Ingredients and cooking: ₹45 to 60
  • Packaging: ₹8 to 12
  • Delivery, self-ride and batched: ₹10 to 20 a drop
  • Delivery app, per order: ₹30 to 50, maths breaks
The math

₹120 price minus about ₹80 cost is roughly ₹40 a meal, before your own time. Positive only if you batch many drops in one lane. A single delivery across town loses money.

Hard truth

The real unit is not one customer. It is one cluster. 5 to 10 dabbas on a single trip, or the economics simply do not work.

5Why now?

Free AI tools let one student build the app, the brand and the order flow in days. Three years ago this needed a paid developer.

UPI means you take money with zero gateway and zero paperwork.

Bengaluru's PG and migrant crowd keeps growing, and tiffin demand stays steady, with 5,800 plus listings.

Healthier-than-daily-Swiggy is a rising, named want, which is exactly why Swiggy built EatRight.

6The riskiest assumption
The belief that kills it

That Ravi will move his dinners from easy Swiggy to one small unknown cook, and keep doing it week after week. And that you can gather enough nearby orders to deliver at a profit.

Test it this week

Make next week's one-page menu. Walk into ONE PG or one lane. Get 5 to 10 people to pre-pay ₹100 for ONE real dinner you deliver this week. Cook it, hand-deliver it.

Win condition

5 plus paid, same-lane deliveries, and half of them ask for the next day. If people will not pre-pay ₹100 for one real meal, no app will fix that.

The verdict
1

Build it, but change one thing. Stop selling to "one customer" and sell to one PG cluster. Win a single lane of 8 to 10 dinners first.

2

The app is the easy part. Spend your six weeks proving repeat orders in one cluster, not polishing features.

3

If one lane will not pre-pay ₹100 for a real meal twice in a week, swap to a sharper version: a fixed small weekly menu for one office floor or one PG, pre-paid, no daily choice. Easier to cook, deliver and keep.

Better to hear it now than on Demo Day.
