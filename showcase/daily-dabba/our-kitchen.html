Our kitchen

The kind of care a hired cook rarely gives.

Daily Dabba started with a simple, slightly stubborn idea: nobody far from home should have to eat sad food.

So we built a kitchen that cooks the way a home does. Vegetables and meat are bought fresh each morning, not stocked for the week. The meals are made in small batches, tasted before they leave, and packed warm.

There is no central factory churning out trays of the same gravy. There are people who cook like they are feeding their own family, because the difference between fine and wonderful is almost always just care.

What we promise

Five things we will not cut corners on.

01

Cooked the same day

Your dabba is made the morning it reaches you. No three day old curry, ever.

02

Fresh ingredients

Produce and meat bought each morning, not pulled from a deep freezer.

03

A clean kitchen

Hygiene is not a slogan here. The place your food is cooked is kept the way you would keep your own.

04

Honest portions

Enough to actually fill you up after a long morning. No styled, half empty boxes.

05

Real home taste

Spiced like home, not like a restaurant trying to impress. Comfort over flash.

06

We own our mistakes

A bad dabba is on us. Tell us and we make it right, simply.

You cannot fake food that tastes like someone cared while cooking it.
