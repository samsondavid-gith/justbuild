The person

Ananya, 29, Bengaluru. Loves sarees, wants real handloom and not powerloom, and quietly dreads that the blouse will never fit. Buys for festivals and work.

The moment it bites

At the shop she cannot tell handloom from powerloom, the price feels made up, and the blouse from the local tailor comes back tight at the arms. Online, she worries it will not be the real thing.

What she does today

Buys from a local shop or a trusted brand to feel safe, gets the blouse stitched separately, and simply hopes the fit works out.

What I will build

A web app for a handloom saree house: choose the weave, see a live itemised price, pick the drape, and order a made-to-measure blouse, all from named weavers.

1Is the problem real?

The pain is real. Shops sell powerloom as handloom, prices feel arbitrary, and a blouse stitched separately so often does not fit.

Big market. India saree is about $6 billion heading toward $10 billion, cotton leads, and working women are draping more, not less.

Handloom is reviving: buyers want real, traceable, sustainable weaves, and transparency and fair weaver pay are rising demands.

Honest flag

Handloom plus a story plus some transparency already exists: Suta, Taneira, iTokri, weaver co-ops. Your edge is the combination, custom weave plus drape plus a fitted blouse plus an open price, not handloom alone.

3 questions for Ananya
About last time, not "would you use an app"
  1. Show me the last saree you bought. Could you tell if it was real handloom, and did you know what you were paying for?
  2. The last blouse you had stitched, did it fit the first time? What happened if it did not?
  3. Have you ever bought a saree online from a brand you did not know? What made you trust it, or stop you?

3Can a student build it in six weeks?
Smallest version

One page. A few real handloom sarees from one cluster, each with weave, region and a price breakup, plus a blouse-measurement form and an order request.

Build for real

Catalogue from a data file (weave, region, price, weaver share). A live price calculator. A simple measurement form. An order or enquiry. No big inventory needed to demo.

Fake or do by hand

Inventory: hold a tiny range or buy-to-order from one weaver. Tailoring: partner with ONE tailor, handle measurements by hand at first. Delivery: by hand or courier.

Cut for now

No big inventory, no national logistics, no dozen weaves, no payment gateway, no login. One weave, one tailor, five real orders.

Reality check

The app is the easy part. The real venture is blouse FIT online and PROVING the saree is handloom. Nail those two on 5 real orders before adding anything.

4The money on one customer
What she pays

Cotton from about ₹1,600, Chanderi about ₹3,200, silk ₹6,500 and up. Blouse tailoring is roughly ₹300 to ₹800.

What it costs me
  • Saree, bought from the weaver
  • Blouse stitching to the tailor
  • Free first alteration, a real cost
  • Shipping and the odd return
The math

Direct from the weaver gives a real margin and fair pay, and the blouse adds value. Capital is light enough for a student to actually start, unlike gold.

Watch this

Your real cost risk is alterations and returns from bad fit. The free-alteration promise must be budgeted for. Keep the range tiny until fit is dialled in.

Handloom is reviving on the back of sustainability and traceability, exactly your story.

Working women are draping more, and online ethnic wear is growing, with buyers now expecting transparency.

The revival also brings funded brands like Suta and Taneira. Why now works for YOU because you are smaller, custom and hyper-transparent, not because the space is empty.

6The riskiest assumption
The belief that kills it

That a woman will buy a handloom saree AND trust a made-to-measure blouse to fit, online, from a brand new name, sight unseen. Fit and authenticity are the twin risks.

Test it this week

Get 5 real strangers to actually order one saree plus a measured blouse. Track two things: did they believe it was real handloom, and did the blouse fit first time?

Win condition

5 strangers pay, receive, and 4 of 5 say the blouse fit and the saree felt real. If fit and trust hold at five, you have a venture.

The verdict
1

A real, feasible wedge, unlike gold. Handloom direct from the weaver, with a custom drape, a fitted blouse and an open price, is a combination the big players do not quite offer. The market is large and the capital is light enough to start.

2

But the venture is not the saree, it is the fit and the trust. Powerloom-as-handloom and a blouse that does not fit are exactly the doubts you must beat, online, as an unknown. That is operationally hard.

3

Start narrow and prove it: one weave region, a made-to-measure blouse with free alteration, the weaver named and the price open. Get 5 strangers to order, then measure fit and belief. Win those two and Pallu is real.

Better to hear it now than on Demo Day.
