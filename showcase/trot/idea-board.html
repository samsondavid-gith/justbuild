The person

Rohan, 31, Bengaluru. Works long hours and has a Labrador, Simba. Feels guilty when Simba misses a walk, and is nervous about handing him to someone he does not know.

The moment it bites

It is 6pm, a work crisis, and Simba has not been out since morning. The building help is busy. The one time Rohan tried a random walker, he had no idea where they went or if Simba was okay.

What he does today

Relies on himself, his partner, or the occasional building help. On bad days, Simba just misses a walk. He would never hand him to a stranger he cannot see.

What I will build

An app to book a vetted walker on demand, follow the walk live on a map, and get a photo report when Simba is home safe.

1Is the problem real?

The pain is real. Busy urban pet parents cannot walk their dogs daily, and handing the dog to a stranger with no visibility is genuinely scary.

Big and fast-growing. India pet services are heading from about $485 million to over $1 billion by 2030, with 30 million-plus pet dogs and pets treated as family.

The lane looks open: there is no dominant Indian on-demand dog-walking app. Wag and Rover are US players, not here.

Honest flag

An open lane can also mean a hard market. Even in the US, dog-walking marketplaces like Wag and Rover struggled with thin margins. Walking alone is a tough standalone business.

3 questions for Rohan
About last time, not "would you use an app"
  1. The last time Simba missed a walk, what actually stopped you, and what did you do instead?
  2. Have you ever let someone else walk him? Who, and what made you trust them or not?
  3. What would you pay for a walk, and would you pay for one every single day?

3Can a student build it in six weeks?
Smallest version

One neighbourhood, 3 to 5 genuinely vetted walkers, a booking form, a live-location share link, and a photo report by message. That is a real test.

Build for real

A booking app, walker profiles, a simple live map (or shared location to start), and a photo-report flow. Match walkers to parents by hand at first.

Fake or do by hand

Tracking can start as shared live location, not a custom GPS engine. Matching, vetting and payments by hand. Insurance: a basic policy or a clear liability agreement to begin.

Cut for now

No city-wide coverage, no automated dispatch, no payment gateway, no in-app GPS engine, no sitting or grooming yet. One block, a few walkers.

Reality check

The app is the easy part. The venture is a reliable supply of vetted walkers and trust, a classic two-sided cold start. Nail one neighbourhood with zero incidents and high repeat first.

4The money on one customer
What they pay

A single walk is roughly ₹150 to ₹400 in metros. A daily walk pack is where the real money is.

What it costs me
  • Walker payout, the big one
  • Vetting and insurance
  • Support when things go wrong
  • Getting that first customer
The math

You keep a take rate, maybe 20 to 30 percent, of each walk. Margins are thin, so it only works on repeat, daily packs and neighbourhood density. One-off walks barely pay.

Hard truth

On-demand walking has famously thin unit economics, see Wag and Rover. Do not assume volume fixes it. Density, retention and packs are the only path to money.

5Why now?

AI, no-code and phone GPS make a tracked, photo-reported walk easy to build in days.

Pet humanization plus double-income, nuclear families means real demand and a real willingness to pay.

No Indian player owns on-demand dog walking yet, so the category is wide open.

Honest flag

"No one owns it" can also mean no one has made it work here yet. The demand is real, but your model must be tighter than the US incumbents that struggled.

6The riskiest assumption
The belief that kills it

That a pet parent will hand their dog to a stranger booked through a brand-new app, AND that you can keep a reliable supply of vetted walkers, AND make money at thin per-walk margins.

Test it this week

In ONE neighbourhood, get 5 real pet parents to book and pay for a vetted walk, and recruit 3 vetted walkers. Track zero incidents, and whether parents rebook.

Win condition

5 parents pay, feel safe thanks to live tracking and a photo, and rebook within a week, with 3 reliable walkers. Repeat plus trust plus zero incidents on one block is a real start.

The verdict
1

Real demand and a genuinely open lane in India. Pet parenting is booming, no one owns on-demand walking here, and trust plus live tracking plus a photo report is a clear, lovable product.

2

But it is a hard business, not a free lane. Trust with your dog, a reliable supply of vetted walkers, liability, and famously thin per-walk economics are exactly why even Wag and Rover struggled. Respect that.

3

Win small and tight: one neighbourhood, a few vetted walkers, daily-walk packs, zero incidents and high repeat. Prove trust and retention on one block before you even whisper the word city.

Better to hear it now than on Demo Day.
