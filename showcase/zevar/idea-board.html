The person

Divya, 30, Bengaluru. Getting married in four months, with a real budget set aside for gold and diamond jewellery, and a quiet fear of being overcharged.

The moment it bites

At the family jeweller the final bill is a mystery: a big making charge she cannot question, no clear gold rate, and a nagging worry about purity. Tanishq is clearer but feels expensive. She does not know who to trust.

What she does today

Visits a few local jewellers and Tanishq, compares vague quotes, leans on a relative's recommendation, and buys in person, because it is gold.

What I will build

An app for a jewellery brand that shows a live, itemised price breakup, BIS and IGI certification on every piece, and a lifetime buyback, so Divya sees exactly what she pays for.

1Is the problem real?

The pain is real. Buyers are widely warned to check the BIS HUID hallmark, the karat, and a fully itemised bill, because vague making charges and purity doubt are common.

And the market is enormous. India jewellery is about $95 billion, with gold roughly half, driven by weddings and festivals.

Trust tools are spreading: HUID codes, in-store Karatmeters, IGI lab certificates and buyback are becoming the norm.

Honest flag

The big brands already solved this. Tanishq itemises every bill, runs a Karatmeter in front of you, and guarantees buyback. Your transparent-pricing-plus-certification-plus-buyback is table stakes, not a wedge.

3 questions for Divya
About last time, not "would you use an app"
  1. Show me the last gold piece your family bought. Can you see the gold rate, making charge and stone value separately on the bill?
  2. What finally made you trust that seller: the brand, a relative, or the hallmark?
  3. The last high-value thing you bought online, how much was it, and what reassured you?

3Can a student build it in six weeks?
Smallest version

One page. A small set of real pieces, each with a photo, the live price breakup (metal plus making plus stones plus GST) and its hallmark and certificate number. A buy or booking request.

Build for real

Catalogue from a data file with weight, purity and rate. A live price calculator that updates with the gold rate. An enquiry or order form. No real gold inventory needed to demo.

Fake or do by hand

Inventory: do NOT hold real gold. Show a tiny real range, or partner with one local jeweller who fulfils. Buyback and payment: explained, handled by hand or by the partner. Certification: show real BIS and IGI examples.

Cut for now

No real gold stock, no own buyback liability, no payment gateway for lakhs, no national delivery of high-value pieces, no login.

Reality check

This is the hardest venture in the cohort. The app is easy. The real business is capital (gold costs lakhs), trust (no one buys gold sight unseen from an unknown), and regulation (BIS, HUID). Build the experience, not a gold shop.

4The money on one customer
What she pays

A gold wedding piece runs ₹30,000 to a few lakh. Even everyday 925 silver is ₹500 to ₹3,000. Making charges are often 8 to 25 percent of the gold value.

What it costs me
  • Real gold inventory: lakhs, locked up
  • Making charges to karigars
  • Hallmarking and certification fees
  • Buyback is a real liability you must fund
The math

One gold piece ties up tens of thousands of rupees of your own money before a single sale, and gold margins are thin. Silver and demi-fine need far less capital and let a student actually start.

Hard truth

You cannot bootstrap a real gold brand as a student. The capital and the buyback promise alone need serious money. Start with silver or demi-fine, or with the experience, not the inventory.

6The riskiest assumption
The belief that kills it

That a stranger will pay tens of thousands of rupees for real gold, online, sight unseen, from a brand they have never heard of, over Tanishq, CaratLane or GIVA.

Test it this week

Do not buy gold. List 5 real, lower-ticket pieces (925 silver or gold-plated) with full transparency, and try to get 5 strangers to actually pay. Or get 5 people to book a paid consultation.

Win condition

5 real strangers pay for a small piece, or book a paid consultation, trusting your brand. If they will not at ₹1,500, they certainly will not at ₹50,000. Earn trust small first.

The verdict
1

Huge market, but trust and capital are the moat, and they are owned. Tanishq, CaratLane and GIVA already offer transparent pricing, certification and buyback. Your stated USP is table stakes, not an edge.

2

This is the hardest venture in the cohort. A student cannot credibly hold gold inventory or fund a buyback promise, and no stranger buys lakhs of gold sight unseen from an unknown brand. Respect that.

3

Build it as an experience and a niche, not a gold shop: a brutally transparent price-breakup and certification flow, on lower-ticket 925 silver or demi-fine, or a consultation that hands off to a trusted jeweller. Win trust at ₹1,500 before dreaming of ₹50,000.

Better to hear it now than on Demo Day.
